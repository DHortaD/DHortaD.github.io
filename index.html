Short bio

I am currently a Flatiron research fellow in the Galactic Dynamics group at the Center for Computational Astrophysics (CCA) at the Flatiron Institute in New York City.

I obtained my PhD in 2022 at the Astrophysics Research Institute (Liverpool, UK).

I am what people call a “spanish-geordie”.

You can reach me at dhortadarrington@flatironinstitute.org

Research

I am primarily interested in the formation and evolution of the Milky Way and its Globular Cluster system, and how we can apply this knowledge to further understand how other galaxies in the Universe form. Having said that, recently I have also become more interested in dynamics, statistics/machine learning, and Dark Matter.

I primarily work with big data from either large-scale surveys of the Milky Way (e.g., Gaia, APOGEE, GALAH) and/or data from sophisticated cosmological numerical simulations

You can reach me at dhortadarrington@flatironinstitute.org
